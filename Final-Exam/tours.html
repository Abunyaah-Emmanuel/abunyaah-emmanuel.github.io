Cozumel Island in Mexico

COZUMEL — A a mostly undeveloped Mexican island in the Caribbean Sea, is a popular cruise ship port of call famed for its scuba diving. At Arrecifes de Cozumel National Park, there's diving spots around a section of the Mesoamerican Reef and the Museo Subacuático de Arte’s submerged sculptures. Chankanaab is an eco park surrounding a lagoon with underwater caverns, home to dolphins, manatees and sea turtles!

Features of the Island
Area: 478 km²
Location: Caribbean Sea
Total islands: 2
Highest point: 14m
Population: 100,000 (2011)
